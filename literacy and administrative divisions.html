LITERACY

According to the 2011 census, Telangana's literacy rate is 66.46%. Male literacy and female literacy are 74.95% and 57.92%, respectively.Hyderabad district has the highest literacy rate with 80.96% and Mahabubnagar district has the lowest with 56.06%.In a 2019 report, the Key Indicators of Household Social Consumption on Education in India, by the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation, Telangana has a literacy rate of 72.8% which is the fourth lowest among large states. Administrative divisions

The state is divided into 33 districts. The latest two new districts, Mulugu and Narayanpet, were formed on 17 February 2019.The districts are divided into 70 revenue divisions which are further divided into 584 mandals.There are a total of 10,909 revenue villages and 12,769 gram panchayats in the state.
